Startup Procedure

Run these in order at the start of a service. Each step assumes the one before it is done.

1

Power the Racks & the ATEM Panel

Rack · brings up venue devices, rack gear, and the switcher panel
  1. Power on the Furman — this powers all the venue devices.
  2. Power on the Middle Atlantic power conditioner.
  3. Boot the Resi decoder and the console interface.
  4. Physically reconnect power to the ATEM Advanced Panel. The venue unplugs it between services and it has no power switch — it boots on connection. Do it early so it's up by step 3.
2

Connect the Elevation Macbooks

AJA ROI stack under the desk · match by SDI cable colour
SDI cableComputerLands on
WhiteResolume Mac (EC2)IN 35
YellowCG2 Mac (EC3)IN 36
OrangeCG1 Mac (EC4)IN 37
Box labels are pending — cable colour is the interim reference. Verify in step 5.
3

Restore ATEM Profile 44

ATEM 2 M/E Advanced Panel 20 · reloads Elevation mapping, prefs & macros
  1. Press PROFILE, then the right arrow to the profiles page.
  2. Turn the soft knob to Profile 44.
  3. Press RESTORE.
Orange text above the slot confirms it loaded. The manual claims a 10-profile limit; the firmware holds more and 44 was saved on this panel — trust it's there.
4

Recall Videohub Macros

Videohub 40×40 12G front panel · macro lights green, TAKE applies

Standard startup — Elevation macros, one at a time:

STREAM → TAKE  then  CAM → TAKE

After a dicey rental — River first. Restore the venue's general state before ours: MON → TAKE · DECK → TAKE · EDIT → TAKE, then run the Elevation macros on top.
5

Verify on the Videohub Display

Front-panel video preview · after firing the macros

Confirm each Macbook landed on the right input — the picture on IN 35 / 36 / 37 matches white → Resolume, yellow → CG2, orange → CG1. If two are swapped, re-seat the HDMI at the AJA stack per step 2 and re-check.

Signal Flow

Every path is in one of two lanes. Which lane the broken destination is on tells you whether the ATEM is even in play.

Lane A — Switched Program Paths

Sources pass through an ATEM M/E, come back out through the Videohub loopback, and land on a destination. An M/E problem can only ever break a Lane A destination.

source → ATEM M/E → Videohub loopback → destination

ME1 → Makito capture (always Cam 1) · ME2 → Lobby · ME3 → Main Screen (the live switch) · ME4 → utility, expands raw Resolume full-screen via DVE Key 1.

Lane B — Direct Crosspoints

Straight through the Videohub — the ATEM is never involved. Ground panels (raw Resolume, IN 35), confidence monitors, and the Sonifex audio de-embeds all live here.

source → Videohub crosspoint → destination
Fastest triage question: is the broken destination Lane A or Lane B? Lane B rules out the switcher instantly — go straight to the crosspoint.

Diagnose a Symptom

Find the symptom, work the list top to bottom. Each list is ordered by likelihood × how fast it is to check.

Main screen is black or frozen Lane A
  1. Check ME3 program on the panel — is a live source actually cut on the bottom bus?
  2. Check the loopback — confirm the ME3 output crosspoint back through the Videohub still points at the Main Screen output.
  3. Fade-to-black — make sure FTB isn't engaged on ME3.
  4. If the panel itself is dark, it lost power — the venue unplugs it. Reconnect (startup step 1) and restore Profile 44.
Lyrics missing during worship Lane A
  1. ME3 Key 1 (luma, CG1 centre) should be on-air for local worship — check the KEY 1 bus button.
  2. During Uplink, lyrics ride Key 2 (top-right) instead — confirm the right key for the moment you're in.
  3. No fill at all? Confirm CG1 is alive on IN 37 via the Videohub preview — orange SDI cable at the AJA stack.
Ground panels dark, main screen fine Lane B
  1. This is a direct crosspoint path — the ATEM is not involved; don't touch the switcher.
  2. Confirm raw Resolume is alive on IN 35 (white SDI cable, Videohub preview).
  3. Re-fire the Elevation macros: STREAM → TAKE, CAM → TAKE — a rental may have stolen the crosspoint.

Three of ten symptom cards shown — the full set ports over from the live site in Phase 1.

Design Notes

What this mockup commits to, what it borrows from the Production Brand Guide, and where it deliberately deviates.

Dark by Default, Light on Demand

The guide's thesis — show up in the dark, keep it dark, low is our pro — is also the room this site is read in, so the dark stage is the default. But this is a reference, and readability outranks style: the switch (top right) flips to a light background for daylight and bright-FOH use, and remembers the choice. On paper, every accent deepens so it stays legible; the rules don't change, only the tokens.

Written for Pros

This site documents this room, not the craft. It assumes the reader already knows what an M/E, a crosspoint, or a gain stage is — no page ever trains from the ground up. Content earns its place by answering "what do I do at this desk, right now."

Accents behave like tally lights: dots, eyebrows, glows, and focus rings — never floods, never body copy. On the light background each accent shifts to a mid-depth version of itself — dark enough to hold on paper, bright enough to still read as the brand neon. The blue→green→pink gradient is reserved for platform-level moments (the wordmark rule, home).

The Mark

The three-square logomark rides with the wordmark in the rail and on home, rebuilt as vector from the brand asset so it stays crisp at any size. Per the guide it's scaled to the x-height of the lowercase logotype and never changes colour — the guide sanctions the mark on both black and white, so only the wordmark text flips with the background.

Type

Two jobs, two faces, one family underneath. Headings and brand labels keep the guide's voice: Eina 01 semibold, title case (Eina is licensed, so not embedded — where it isn't installed, headings resolve to the same Helvetica family as everything else). Reading text runs Helvetica Now Text → Helvetica Neue at regular weight — the guide's light body weight was tried and dropped; it hampered readability at reference sizes, and on this site readability wins every tie. Lowercase stays reserved for the wordmark.

Platform-First

On a phone the site carries a bottom tab bar and safe-area padding — it reads as an app, and is one "Add to Home Screen" away from launching like one (a real manifest + offline cache is a roadmap phase). On desktop the same routes hang off a persistent rail, so the whole tree is always one click away.

What Stayed from v1

The bones the team already liked: hash-routed subpages, card menus, symptom accordions, numbered startup steps, mono crosspoints, and content that leads with the action instead of a qualifying paragraph.
