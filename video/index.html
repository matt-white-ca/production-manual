Startup Procedure

Run these in order at the start of a service. Each step assumes the one before it is done.

1

Power the Racks & the ATEM Panel

Rack · brings up venue devices, rack gear, and the switcher panel
  1. Power on the Furman — this powers all the venue devices.
  2. Power on the Middle Atlantic power conditioner.
  3. Boot the Resi decoder and the console interface.
  4. Physically reconnect power to the ATEM Advanced Panel. The venue unplugs it between services and it has no power switch — it boots on connection. Do it early so it's up by step 3.
2

Connect the Elevation Macbooks

AJA ROI stack under the desk · match by SDI cable colour
SDI cableComputerLands on
WhiteResolume Mac (EC2)IN 35
YellowCG2 Mac (EC3)IN 36
OrangeCG1 Mac (EC4)IN 37
Box labels are pending — cable colour is the interim reference. Verify in step 5.
3

Restore ATEM Profile 44

ATEM 2 M/E Advanced Panel 20 · reloads Elevation mapping, prefs & macros
  1. Press PROFILE, then the right arrow to the profiles page.
  2. Turn the soft knob to Profile 44.
  3. Press RESTORE.
Orange text above the slot confirms it loaded. The manual claims a 10-profile limit; the firmware holds more and 44 was saved on this panel — trust it's there.
4

Recall Videohub Macros

Videohub 40×40 12G front panel · macro lights green, TAKE applies

Standard startup — Elevation macros, one at a time:

STREAM → TAKE  then  CAM → TAKE

After a dicey rental — River first. Restore the venue's general state before ours: MON → TAKE · DECK → TAKE · EDIT → TAKE, then run the Elevation macros on top.
5

Verify on the Videohub Display

Front-panel video preview · after firing the macros

Confirm each Macbook landed on the right input — the picture on IN 35 / 36 / 37 matches white → Resolume, yellow → CG2, orange → CG1. If two are swapped, re-seat the HDMI at the AJA stack per step 2 and re-check.

Signal Flow

Every path is in one of two lanes. Which lane the broken destination is on tells you whether the ATEM is even in play.

Lane A — Switched Program Paths

Source → ATEM M/E → Videohub loopback → destination. An M/E problem can only ever break a Lane A destination.

ME1 · capture bus
ME1 PGM → Videohub IN 21 (loopback) → OUT 19 → Makito Encoder → Playback capture
ME2 · top bus
ME2 PGM → Videohub IN 22 (loopback) → OUT 31 → Lobby distribution → Lobby TVs
ME3 · bottom bus, live switch
ME3 PGM → Videohub IN 23 (loopback) → OUT 22 → LED Controller → Main Screen
ME4 · utility bus, not a destination
Raw Resolume (ATEM IN 12) → ME4 DVE Key 1 → selectable as "Resolume" source on ME1 / ME2 / ME3 → full-screen
Lane B — Direct Crosspoint Paths

Source → Videohub crosspoint directly → destination. The ATEM is not involved.

Resolume Mac · EC2, white AJA ROI
Resolume Mac → Videohub IN 35 → OUT 23 → Ground Panels (raw / untreated)
CG2 Mac · EC3, yellow AJA ROI
CG2 Mac → Videohub IN 36 → OUT 24 → Top Confidence Monitor
Clock Mac · BMD Mini Converter, Sunday hijack
Clock Mac → Videohub IN 13 ("Resolume 1") → OUT 25 → Bottom Confidence Monitor
Resi Decoder · EC1
Resi Decoder → Videohub IN 34 → OUT 18 → DEMBED 1 → Console Dante 1–12
CG1 Mac · EC4, orange AJA ROI
CG1 Mac → Videohub IN 37 → OUT 20 → DEMBED 2 → Console Dante 25/26
Fastest triage question: is the broken destination Lane A or Lane B? Lane B rules out the switcher instantly — go straight to the crosspoint.

Diagnose a Symptom

Open the symptom that matches what you're seeing. Each one lists the exact hop sequence and where to look first — worked top to bottom in order.

Main Screen — black, frozen, or wrong source Lane A
ME3 PGM → Videohub IN 23 → OUT 22 → LED Controller → Main Screen
  1. Confirm ME3 is actually delegated to the panel's bottom bus and the intended source is taken on PGM.
  2. Check the ATEM multiview for ME3 PGM — if it's correct there, the fault is downstream of the switcher.
  3. Verify the Videohub crosspoint OUT 22 is still patched from IN 23 (not accidentally re-routed).
  4. Check LED Controller input status/signal lock, then cabling from Videohub to controller.
If ME3 PGM itself is wrong on the multiview, the problem is upstream — check whichever source is cut in (see Input Cross-Points).
Lyrics missing, wrong, or mispositioned on Main Screen Lane A
CG1 Mac / ProPresenter → AJA ROI (orange) → ATEM IN 9 → ME3 Key 1 (centre) / Key 2 (top, Link) → ME3 PGM
  1. Confirm ProPresenter is actually outputting on the CG1 Macbook.
  2. Check the orange-tagged AJA ROI link status for that run.
  3. Confirm ATEM IN 9 shows signal on the multiview.
  4. On ME3: centre lyrics use Key 1, top/"Link" lyrics use Key 2 — confirm the correct key is enabled and its luma clip/gain and mask haven't drifted.
  5. Confirm key layer priority still sits above fill in the ME3 keyer stack.
Ground Panels — wrong image, frozen, or dark Lane B
Resolume Mac / EC2 → AJA ROI (white) → Videohub IN 35 → OUT 23 → Ground Panels
  1. The ATEM is not in this path — don't spend time on ME3/ME4, this feed is a direct crosspoint.
  2. Confirm Resolume is actually outputting on the Resolume Macbook and it's the raw/untreated composition (not a full-screen DVE version — that's ME4's job, not this feed's).
  3. Check the white-tagged AJA ROI link status.
  4. Confirm Videohub crosspoint OUT 23 is still patched from IN 35.
  5. Check the LED Controller's input for the ground panels and cabling from Videohub.
Resolume graphic looks small or wrong size when used elsewhere Lane A
Raw Resolume (ATEM IN 12) → ME4 DVE Key 1 → cut in as "ME4" on ME1/2/3
  1. This symptom means the operator likely cut to the raw EC2/Resolume source directly instead of ME4.
  2. ME4's only job is to take that raw feed and expand it full-screen via its DVE Key 1 — confirm Key 1 is enabled on ME4.
  3. On whichever M/E needs the full-screen graphic, confirm the source selected is ME4, labelled "Resolume" — not the raw input.
Lobby TVs — wrong image or dark Lane A
ME2 PGM → Videohub IN 22 → OUT 31 → Lobby TVs
  1. Confirm ME2 is delegated to the panel's top bus and has the intended source on PGM.
  2. Check ME2 PGM on the multiview.
  3. Confirm Videohub crosspoint OUT 31 is still patched from IN 22.
  4. Check the lobby TV distribution path and individual sets.
Recording / Makito capture blank or wrong Lane A
ME1 PGM → Videohub IN 21 → OUT 19 → Makito Encoder
  1. Confirm ME1 PGM on the multiview shows the intended capture source.
  2. Confirm Videohub crosspoint OUT 19 is still patched from IN 21.
  3. Check Makito encoder status/link independently of the video path.
Top Confidence Monitor wrong Lane B
CG2 Mac / EC3 → AJA ROI (yellow) → Videohub IN 36 → OUT 24 → Top Confidence Monitor
  1. This bypasses the ATEM entirely — check CG2's own output first.
  2. Check the yellow-tagged AJA ROI link status.
  3. Confirm Videohub crosspoint OUT 24 is still patched from IN 36.
Bottom Confidence Monitor wrong / clock not showing Sunday Lane B
Clock Mac + BMD Mini Converter → Videohub IN 13 ("Resolume 1") → OUT 25 → Bottom Confidence Monitor
  1. This port is dual-purpose: it's labelled "Resolume 1" but is hijacked by the Clock Macbook on Sundays. On other days this monitor reflects the actual River Resolume 1 SDI at FOH, not a clock.
  2. If the clock isn't showing on a Sunday, confirm the BMD Mini Converter is connected and the Clock Macbook is set to that output.
  3. Confirm Videohub crosspoint OUT 25 is still patched from IN 13.
No console audio from Resi or CG1 Lane B
Resi Decoder (EC1) → IN 34 → OUT 18 → DEMBED 1 → Dante 1–12
CG1 Mac (EC4) → IN 37 → OUT 20 → DEMBED 2 → Dante 25/26
  1. These audio paths de-embed from the same SDI feeds used for video — if picture is fine but audio is missing, the fault is isolated to the Sonifex AVN DIO 2 embedder/de-embedder stage or the Dante routing, not the SDI path.
  2. Check Sonifex AVN DIO 2 channel assignment and status for DEMBED 1 (Resi) or DEMBED 2 (CG1).
  3. Confirm the console's Dante patch still has channels 1–12 (Resi) / 25–26 (CG1) assigned to the correct input strip.
